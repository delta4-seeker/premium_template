Frequently Asked Questions

What is Nano Syllabus and how does it differ from a standard LLM?
Nano Syllabus is a platform for creating Pedagogically-Aligned Small Language Models (SLMs). Unlike generic LLMs (which are trained on the public web), our SLMs are trained *exclusively* on your institution's curriculum, rubrics, and faculty logic. This guarantees consistent, predictable, and academically validated instructional behavior.
Where is our data stored, and what are the governance options?
We prioritize institutional data control. Nano Syllabus models are designed for local deployment on your private cloud, university servers, or air-gapped infrastructure. Your curriculum, institutional IP, and student interaction data *never* leave your controlled environment.
How does the platform ensure models do not "hallucinate" or deviate from the curriculum?
Our process uses Controlled Distillation and Multi-Layer Validation. The models are inherently smaller and only trained on the specific pedagogical blueprint provided by your faculty. This focused design significantly reduces the risk of generalization and ensures reasoning is traceable and curriculum-aligned.
What is the typical time commitment from faculty and IT staff to get started?
Faculty involvement is focused on Step 1: Pedagogical Specification, where they define the instructional rules and rubrics (the "blueprint"). Once the blueprint is complete, our automated pipeline handles the model training and validation. Deployment is low-friction, integrating seamlessly with existing LMS and cloud tools.
Can Nano Syllabus handle both K-12 instruction and graduate-level research models?
Yes. The platform is designed to distill *any* pedagogical logic. This means you can create one SLM optimized for structured K-12 math explanations and another for complex, highly specific graduate-level literature analysis. The strength lies in the model's precise alignment to your defined discipline-specific logic.
